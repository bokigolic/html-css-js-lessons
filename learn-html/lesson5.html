Lesson 5: Images and Multimedia

🖼️ Why Images and Multimedia Matter

Visual content brings your web pages to life. Whether it’s a picture, a video, or a piece of music—media helps engage visitors and deliver richer experiences.

📷 Adding Images with <img>

The <img> tag is used to display images on your page:

<img src="cat.jpg" alt="Cute cat smiling" />
  • src – the image file location (relative or absolute)
  • alt – alternative text (shown if image fails or for screen readers)
<img src="sunset.jpg" alt="Sunset over the ocean" width="400" />

📱 Making Images Responsive

To make sure your images adapt to different screen sizes:

<img src="landscape.jpg" alt="Beautiful landscape" style="max-width: 100%; height: auto;" />

This ensures the image scales down on smaller screens but never stretches.

🖼️ Example: Mini Image Gallery

<h2>My Travel Gallery</h2>
<img src="rome.jpg" alt="Colosseum in Rome" width="300" />
<img src="paris.jpg" alt="Eiffel Tower at night" width="300" />
<img src="nyc.jpg" alt="New York skyline" width="300" />

🎥 Embedding Video with <video>

Use the <video> tag to add videos to your page:

<video width="320" height="240" controls>
  <source src="intro.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
  Your browser does not support the video tag.
</video>
  • controls – shows play/pause, volume, etc.
  • autoplay, loop, muted – optional video behaviors

🎧 Embedding Audio with <audio>

Similar to video, you can add audio files:

<audio controls>
  <source src="soundtrack.mp3" type="audio/mpeg" />
  Your browser does not support the audio element.
</audio>

🧪 Practice: Multimedia About Your Hobby

Create a short multimedia section:

<h2>🎮 My Hobby: Gaming</h2>
<img src="console.jpg" alt="Gaming console" />
<video src="gameplay.mp4" controls></video>
<audio src="game-music.mp3" controls></audio>

📌 Summary

  • Use <img> for images. Don’t forget alt!
  • Make images responsive with style="max-width: 100%"
  • Use <video> and <audio> for rich media
  • Add controls to give users playback access
