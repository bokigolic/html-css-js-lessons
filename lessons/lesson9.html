🧠 What is Semantic HTML?

Semantic HTML uses tags that describe meaning and context—like `

`, `
`, and `
`. This helps browsers, developers, and assistive technologies understand your content better.

Why it matters: Enhances SEO, accessibility, and code organization.

📦 Common Semantic Tags

  • <header> – top section of page or area
  • <nav> – navigation links
  • <main> – main unique content
  • <section> – thematic grouping
  • <article> – independent piece (e.g. blog post)
  • <aside> – side notes, related links
  • <footer> – bottom section of page or area

🖼️ Example: Page Structure

<header>
  <h1>My Blog</h1>
</header>

<nav>
  <a href="#">Home</a>
  <a href="#">Posts</a>
</nav>

<main>
  <article>
    <h2>Learning HTML</h2>
    <p>HTML is the skeleton of the web.</p>
  </article>

  <aside>
    <h3>Tip</h3>
    <p>Use semantic tags for better structure!</p>
  </aside>
</main>

<footer>
  <p>© 2025 LearnFrontend.dev</p>
</footer>

✅ Why It’s Valuable

  • 🔍 Better SEO and indexing
  • 🧏 Improved screen-reader navigation
  • 🛠️ Easier for devs to read & maintain
  • 📐 Clear document structure/outline
