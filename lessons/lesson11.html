🧠 What are Accessibility & SEO?

Accessibility means making your site usable for everyone – including people with disabilities. SEO means helping search engines understand your content so it appears in search results.

💡 Tip: Accessible sites often rank better in search engines 🎯

🔖 Semantic & ARIA Tags

  • alt text for images
  • aria-label or aria-hidden for icons/buttons
  • role="navigation", role="main", etc.
  • Proper heading structure (<h1> → <h2> → …)
<img src="logo.png" alt="Company Logo">
<button aria-label="Open menu">☰</button>
<nav role="navigation">…</nav>
💡 Make sure decorative images use alt="" to skip them in screen readers.

📦 SEO Meta Basics

  • <html lang="en"> – defines language for screen-readers and SEO
  • <meta charset="UTF-8"> – ensures correct text encoding
  • <meta name="description"> – preview text in search results
  • title – shown in browser tab & search engine title
<meta name="description" content="Short, clear overview of page content">
<title>Lesson 11: Accessibility & SEO Basics</title>

🏗️ Headings & Landmark Roles

Use headings for structure and define landmarks to help navigation:

<header role="banner">…</header>
<main role="main">…</main>
<footer role="contentinfo">…</footer>
⚠️ Ensure you only have one <h1> on a page for clarity.

🧪 Example: Accessible Image & Links

<img src="team.jpg" alt="Three team members smiling" />
<a href="about.html" aria-label="Learn more about our team">About Us</a>
💡 Screen readers will read the aria-label instead of link text.

📌 Summary

  • Always use meaningful alt text or aria-hidden when appropriate
  • Include landmark roles and logical heading hierarchy
  • Ensure meta tags: charset, description, viewport
  • Language, structure & readability help both users and SEO
